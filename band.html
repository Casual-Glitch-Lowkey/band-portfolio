My Favorite Album Releases

OK Computer (1997)
A bold, atmospheric album that feels futuristic and anxious at the same time. The songwriting is detailed and emotional, and the production layers make it rewarding to re-listen.
Kid A (2000)
A creative left turn that leans into electronic textures and strange soundscapes. It’s less “rock band” and more “mood + experimentation,” but it still hits hard.
In Rainbows (2007)
Warm, human, and beautifully paced. The performances feel intimate, and the album balances catchy moments with subtle, haunting ones.
